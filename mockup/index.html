Help & FAQ

Getting Started
What is Immagini Finance?
Immagini is a personal finance tracker for Australians. It lets you categorise your bank transactions, set budgets, track savings goals, tag tax claims, and get AI-powered insights — all in one place.
How do I navigate between pages?
Use the tabs at the bottom: Home for your transactions, Insights for charts and analysis, Chat to ask the AI assistant, Goals for savings tracking, Budget for monthly limits, and Categories to manage your category list. Import is available under Settings (top-right gear icon).
How does the month switcher work?
Tap the ‹ or › arrows next to the month label at the top to move between months. Full transaction data is available for April, May, and June 2026. July shows upcoming bills only.
How do I search my transactions?
Use the search bar above your transactions to find by merchant name, note, or dollar amount. Tap the filter icon (≡) to also filter by category, date range, or amount range — you can combine all at once.
What is the "Safe to spend" bar?
The safe-to-spend bar shows your current balance minus all upcoming recurring bills due this month (like rent, subscriptions, utilities). It tells you what you can comfortably spend without missing a bill.
Categorising Transactions
How do I categorise a transaction?
Tap any transaction row to open its detail panel. Choose a category and subcategory from the list. The app will ask if you want to teach it that keyword so future transactions from the same merchant are categorised automatically.
What is the Split feature?
Split lets you divide a single transaction across multiple categories. For example, a $150 Woolworths shop could be split $100 Grocery + $50 Household. The transaction shows a "Split ×2" badge and a purple left border. You can undo a split at any time from the same panel.
How do keywords work?
When you assign a category, the app can learn a keyword from the merchant name. Next time a transaction with the same keyword appears (e.g. "Woolworths"), it will suggest the same category automatically. You can manage keywords under Categories.
Can I add a note to a transaction?
Yes — tap the transaction to open its panel and type in the note field. Notes appear as a small badge on the transaction row and are also searchable from the search bar.
How do I customise category icons and names?
Go to the Categories tab in the footer. Tap any category to edit its name or tap its icon to choose from the icon library.
Tax & EOFY
How do I tag a transaction as a tax claim?
Tap any transaction to open its panel, then tap the Tax claim button. The transaction gets a green "Tax" badge. You can find all your tax-tagged transactions using the Tax claim filter in the filter panel.
How do I see all my tax claim transactions?
On the Home screen, tap the filter icon and select Tax claim under Tags, then tap Apply. Or go to Insights — the ATO Tax Claim card always shows your tagged transactions for the current month.
How do I export my ATO tax report?
From the Insights page, scroll to the ATO Tax Claim card. Tap ⬇ CSV to download a spreadsheet, or ⬇ PDF to get a print-ready report with a centered table layout, totals, and ATO reference note. You need at least one tax-tagged transaction for the export to be active.
What does the PDF tax report include?
The PDF includes: transaction date, merchant name, category, dollar amount, and any notes you added. It also shows the total claimable amount and a footer noting the report is for ATO records. It opens in a new tab — use your browser's Print → Save as PDF option.
Budgets
How do I set a monthly budget?
Go to the Budget tab. Tap the number field next to any category and type in your monthly limit. The progress bar updates live as you type.
What does "over budget" mean?
When your spending in a category exceeds the monthly limit you set, the bar turns red and shows how much you're over. Under budget shows in green with the remaining amount.
Can I see budget history?
Yes — tap View yearly trend under any category to see month-by-month spending vs. your budget for the past 6 months.
Insights
What does the Compare chart show?
The Compare chart shows your monthly spending per category over the last 6 months. Select one or more categories using the chips above the chart, and toggle between Line and Bar view. Dollar values are shown on data points and on the Y-axis.
Does the Insights page respond to filters?
Yes — the search bar and filter panel on the Insights page are shared with the Home screen. Filtering by category, date, or amount will update the top-merchant list and ATO tax card to reflect only the filtered transactions.
What is the "Category trends" card?
It highlights the top 3 categories with the biggest month-over-month change — useful for spotting where your spending has jumped or dropped compared to last month.
Goals
How do I create a savings goal?
Go to the Goals tab. In this version, goals (like Bali Trip and Emergency Fund) are pre-loaded. In a future version you'll be able to add custom goals with names, targets, and deadlines.
How do I allocate a transfer to a goal?
Tap any transaction in the Transfers category to open the Allocate panel. You can split the transfer amount across one or more savings goals — for example, $500 to Bali and $500 to Emergency Fund.
Importing Data
How do I import my bank transactions?
Go to Settings → Import transactions. Drag and drop or tap to choose a CSV file exported from your bank. Immagini will parse the date, merchant, and amount columns automatically.
What CSV format is supported?
Any CSV with columns for date, description/merchant, and amount (debit or credit). The parser tries common bank formats automatically. If a column isn't detected, a mapping screen will appear.
Which Australian banks are supported?
CommBank, Westpac, ANZ, NAB, Macquarie, ING, and most banks that export standard CSV statements. More formats are added regularly.
AI Assistant
What can I ask the AI assistant?
You can ask anything about your spending — for example: "How much did I spend on coffee this month?", "What's my biggest expense category?", "Am I on track with my budget?", or "Show me all my subscriptions." The AI reads your transaction data to give you personalised answers.
Is my financial data shared with AI servers?
In this prototype, the chat is a demo with pre-set responses. In the live version, your data would be processed securely and never used to train AI models. Privacy controls will be available in Settings.
